Coal Mining Waste Management

Overburden and Spoil

Overburden refers to the soil and rock removed to access coal seams.

Management Strategy: Land reclamation, backfilling, and vegetation.

Coal Rejects/Tailings

Leftover material after coal processing containing impurities.

Management Strategy: Stored in tailings dams, dry stacking, and recycling.

Acid Mine Drainage (AMD)

Acidic water formed when sulfide minerals are exposed to air and water.

Management Strategy: Neutralization with lime, constructed wetlands.

Coal Slurry/Sludge

A mixture of water and coal fines produced during processing.

Management Strategy: Dewatering, safe containment, and reuse of water.

Dust and Particulate Matter

Dust generated during mining and transportation of coal.

Management Strategy: Water sprays, dust collectors, and vegetation.

Methane Emissions

Methane gas released during coal extraction, contributing to greenhouse gases.

Management Strategy: Methane capture, flaring, and ventilation systems.

Heavy Metals and Toxins

Release of toxic substances like mercury, arsenic, and cadmium.

Management Strategy: Water treatment and secure containment.
